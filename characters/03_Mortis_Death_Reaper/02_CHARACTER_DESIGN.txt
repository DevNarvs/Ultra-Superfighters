FRAME-BY-FRAME POSE GUIDE
================================================================================

ROW 0 — IDLE (4 frames, looping)
---------------------------------
F0: Neutral hovering stance. Body upright, very slight forward lean. Scythe
    held vertically in the right hand at the character's side — shaft runs
    along the body, blade curves upward and to the right above the shoulder.
    Left arm hangs at the side, skeletal hand visible with fingers slightly
    curled. Robe drapes naturally, tattered bottom edges hang near y=58 with
    a 1-2px gap underneath suggesting the character floats. Hood points
    upward, interior pure black with two glowing cyan-green eye dots clearly
    visible. Shoulder pauldrons visible on both shoulders. Chain belt with
    gem visible at the waist. Robe tendrils hang still. Calm, ominous pose.

F1: Body drifts UPWARD 1-2px. Robe tendrils begin to sway gently to the
    left (as if moved by an unseen wind). Eye glow pulses slightly brighter
    — add 1 extra pixel of #66FFCC around each eye dot. Scythe tilts very
    slightly. The gap under the robe hem increases by 1px (floating higher).
    Chain gem sways slightly.

F2: Peak of float. Body at highest position (2px above F0). Robe tendrils
    drift further to the left, spread slightly more. Eyes at maximum glow
    brightness — the brightest they get during idle. Scythe tilts a fraction
    more. A sense of weightless, ethereal hovering. The most "supernatural"
    frame of the idle loop.

F3: Body descends back toward F0 position. Robe tendrils begin settling back.
    Eye glow dims back to base #00FFAA. Gap under hem shrinks. Everything
    returns smoothly to F0 for a seamless loop. The motion should feel like
    slow, ghostly breathing — a figure that doesn't breathe but pulses with
    dark energy.

IMPORTANT: The idle animation should convey that this character is NOT
standing — it is HOVERING. The slight up-down float, swaying tendrils,
and eye pulsing create an unsettling, supernatural presence.


ROW 1 — GLIDE (6 frames, looping)
---------------------------------
This character does NOT run with legs. The "run" animation is a forward
GLIDE/FLOAT. No leg movement is visible — the robe conceals everything below
the waist. Movement is conveyed through body lean, robe billowing, and
scythe positioning.

F0: Body leans forward ~8 degrees into the direction of movement. Scythe
    held back in the right hand, shaft trailing behind at ~45 degrees, blade
    pointing back and up. Left arm extended slightly forward, skeletal hand
    reaching ahead. Robe streams behind the body — the tattered tendrils
    stretch out to the left, trailing like ghostly ribbons. Hood leans
    forward with the body. Eyes glow steadily. The character looks like
    a dark specter gliding forward with intent.

F1: Body dips slightly lower (1-2px down). The forward lean deepens a
    fraction. Robe billows more — the fabric creates a wave shape behind,
    like cloth caught in wind. Tendrils stretch further back. Left arm
    position shifts slightly. This is the "lowest" point of the glide bob.

F2: Body rises back up 1px. Robe ripples — the wave shape shifts along the
    trailing fabric. The body is in the passing/transition position. Scythe
    shaft angle adjusts slightly with the body motion. Tendrils begin to
    settle before the next dip.

F3: Mirror lean of F0 with subtle differences. The forward lean continues
    but the body has shifted — left arm pulls back slightly, right arm
    (with scythe) adjusts. Robe billows in a slightly different pattern
    from F0 to prevent the glide from looking too mechanical.

F4: Body dips again (mirror of F1). Different robe billow pattern. The
    tendrils stretch in a slightly different configuration. Creates natural
    variation in the floating motion.

F5: Body rises (mirror of F2). Robe settles slightly. Loops seamlessly
    back to F0.

CRITICAL: NO visible leg movement at any point. The robe bottom maintains
a consistent shape — it does not open to reveal legs. The entire movement
impression comes from the upper body lean, arm positions, robe flow, and
tendril trailing. This character GLIDES like a ghost, not runs like a human.


ROW 2 — JUMP (3 frames, one-shot)
---------------------------------
F0: LAUNCH — body compresses downward. Robe crumples and bunches at the
    bottom. Scythe pulls in close to the body, held vertically against the
    right side. Left arm pulls down. The robe hem drops to touch y=58 fully
    (no floating gap). Hood dips slightly. Eyes maintain glow. The character
    is gathering upward force — even a floating entity needs to coil before
    launching higher.

F1: PEAK — body fully stretched upward, at the highest point. Robe SPREADS
    outward on both sides like dark wings or a cape billowing in the wind.
    The tattered tendrils fan out dramatically — widest silhouette in the
    entire sheet. Scythe raised diagonally upward in the right hand, blade
    pointing up and to the right. Left arm extended out to the side. Eyes
    blaze at maximum brightness (#66FFCC). Hood points sharply upward.
    This should be a dramatic, imposing silhouette — a dark angel ascending.
    Character centered in frame, robe edges can approach frame boundaries.

F2: FALL — body curls inward slightly. Robe wraps around the body like a
    descending shroud — tendrils pull in and stream upward (trailing from
    the downward fall). Scythe points downward, blade aimed at the ground.
    Left arm pulls in close. Eyes dim slightly. Hood tilts forward and down.
    The character descends like a dark omen coming to earth.


ROW 3 — ATTACK 1: Crypt Slash (3 frames, one-shot, 100ms)
---------------------------------
F0: WINDUP — scythe pulls back behind the body to the left. Both hands grip
    the shaft — right hand high, left hand low. Body coils to the left,
    twisting away from the attack direction. Robe follows the twist, fabric
    pulling to the left. Hood turns slightly with the body. Eyes glow
    steadily. Feet/robe bottom remains at y=58. The scythe blade is behind
    the character, ready to sweep forward.

F1: STRIKE — fast horizontal scythe swipe from left to right at chest height.
    The scythe sweeps in a horizontal arc. Blade passes through the space in
    front of the character, reaching approximately 36-38px from the left edge
    of the frame (long reach). Body snaps to the right with the swing. Robe
    whips with the motion. Both hands visible on the shaft mid-swing. Eyes
    flare brighter during the strike. This is the fastest scythe attack —
    a quick reaping slash.

F2: RECOVERY — scythe continues past the strike point, momentum carrying it
    to the right side. Body begins to untwist. Robe settles from the motion.
    Hands shift on the shaft as Mortis pulls the scythe back to neutral.
    Eyes return to normal glow. Return toward idle stance.

IMPORTANT: The scythe blade creates a visible arc/sweep across the frame.
The blade itself should be clearly drawn in each position along the swing
path. Do NOT draw a slash trail — the engine adds that. Only the physical
scythe in its current position.


ROW 4 — ATTACK 2: Reaping Arc (3 frames, one-shot, 100ms)
---------------------------------
F0: WINDUP — scythe raised HIGH overhead with both skeletal hands gripping
    the shaft. Body leans back slightly to counterbalance the raised weapon.
    Robe flares outward at the bottom from the backward lean. Hood tilts back.
    The scythe blade is at the top of the frame, curving to the right, poised
    for a massive downward sweep. Eyes glow intensely — this is a power attack.
    Arms fully extended upward, bone-white hands visible gripping the dark shaft.

F1: STRIKE — scythe sweeps in a massive downward arc from top-right to
    bottom-left. The blade passes through a wide arc covering most of the
    frame's right half. Body lunges forward into the swing, adding weight.
    Robe whips dramatically with the lunging motion. This is the frame with
    maximum reach — the blade tip should be at approximately 44px from the
    left edge and about 40px from the top (low sweep). Eyes blaze. The pose
    should convey tremendous sweeping force — the entire body commits to
    this swing.

F2: RECOVERY — scythe ends its arc at the lower left of the character. Body
    is hunched forward from the lunge. Robe wraps and settles. Mortis begins
    pulling upright, dragging the scythe back. This recovery is noticeably
    slower than Attack 1 — the pose should look like the character needs a
    moment to pull back from such a heavy swing.


ROW 5 — ATTACK 3: Grave Cleave (4 frames, one-shot, 120ms)
---------------------------------
F0: WINDUP — body rises HIGHER than normal (2-3px above usual position).
    Scythe raised above and behind the head with both hands, blade pointing
    backward and up — the highest the scythe ever goes. Body leans back
    dramatically. Robe lifts and billows as if caught in an updraft. Eyes
    blaze maximum bright (#66FFCC). This is the most dramatic windup in the
    sheet — the character is channeling everything into this one strike. A
    momentary pause at the apex before the devastating downswing.

F1: DESCEND — body plunges forward and downward. Scythe begins its massive
    overhead-to-ground swing. The blade sweeps from behind the head in a
    vertical arc coming down toward the ground in front. Mid-swing frame —
    scythe is at roughly 45 degrees, sweeping through. Body weight shifts
    entirely forward. Robe streams upward and behind from the sudden
    downward plunge. Hood dips with the motion.

F2: IMPACT — scythe SLAMS into the ground at maximum forward reach. The
    blade tip touches near the bottom of the frame at full extension —
    approximately 46-48px from the left edge. This is the LONGEST reach of
    any attack in the entire game. Body is crouched over the planted scythe,
    hunched forward. Robe splays outward dramatically from the impact force.
    Eyes blaze. Both hands white-knuckle the shaft. The pose should convey
    earth-shaking impact — even though the character floats, this slam has
    real weight behind it.

F3: RECOVERY — the slowest recovery frame of any attack. Scythe is still
    partially embedded in the ground position. Mortis slowly drags it back,
    body rising sluggishly. One hand releases the shaft to help push
    upright. Robe is still settling from the impact. Eyes dim slightly.
    This is the PUNISH WINDOW — the character is clearly vulnerable. The
    pose should look heavy, committed, and slow to recover. Approximately
    240ms of vulnerability in-game.

IMPORTANT: This is the most committal and dangerous attack. The windup is
telegraphed, the impact is devastating, and the recovery is punishable. The
poses should sell ALL of that — dramatic power AND visible vulnerability.


ROW 6 — CAST: Wraith Bolt (4 frames, one-shot, 120ms)
---------------------------------
F0: PREPARE — scythe is PLANTED in the ground to the right of the character.
    Right hand releases the scythe shaft (it stands upright on its own).
    Both skeletal hands raise to chest level, palms facing forward, fingers
    spread. The character shifts focus from weapon to magic. Robe settles
    into a more vertical drape. Eyes begin to glow brighter. The chain
    gem at the waist also glows slightly.

F1: CHARGE — hands pull apart to shoulder width. Between the skeletal palms,
    a space forms where the spectral skull will manifest (the skull itself is
    NOT drawn — the engine spawns it). Eyes blaze at maximum brightness.
    Fingers curl inward slightly as if containing energy. Green-cyan glow
    appears on the fingertips and palm areas (2-3 pixels of #00FFAA on each
    hand). Body straightens tall. Robe hangs still — the air is heavy with
    dark magic.

F2: RELEASE — left hand thrusts forward toward the right side of the frame,
    palm open, fingers extended. This is the launch point — the engine spawns
    the Wraith Bolt projectile at the left hand position. Right hand pulls
    back. Body twists slightly with the thrust. Robe ripples from the energy
    release. Eyes maintain bright glow. The glow on the extended hand
    intensifies (more #66FFCC pixels).

F3: FOLLOW-THROUGH — left arm still extended but relaxing. Right hand reaches
    back to grab the planted scythe. Body begins to settle. Eye glow dims
    back to normal. Hand glow fades. Wisps of shadow (#221133) trail from
    the extended fingers (2-3 tiny pixels suggesting residual energy).
    Scythe being retrieved from ground position.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT draw the Wraith Bolt skull projectile in any frame. Only
draw the character's body, hands, and the energy glow on the hands. The
spectral skull is a separate sprite spawned by the game engine at the hand
position during F2.


ROW 7 — TAKE HIT (2 frames, one-shot)
---------------------------------
F0: IMPACT — body jerked backward sharply. Hood flips back slightly from the
    force, briefly revealing more of the black void underneath — the shadow
    under the hood is still pure black, but the hood opening is wider,
    showing there truly is NOTHING under there except darkness and eyes.
    Eyes FLICKER — one eye dims to 1px while the other stays lit (asymmetric
    flicker suggests disruption). Scythe knocked to an awkward angle, grip
    loosening. Robe whips forward from the backward momentum. Skeletal hands
    flinch. Chain gem swings. Pain is conveyed not through facial expression
    (there is no face) but through the body jerk, eye flicker, and hood
    displacement.

F1: RECOIL — body leans further backward. Hood settles back slightly but
    still more open than normal. Both eyes dim to a lower glow (#228866
    instead of #00FFAA — sickly, weakened green). One skeletal hand rises
    to clutch the chest area. Scythe droops in the loosened grip. Robe
    continues whipping forward. Robe bottom lifts slightly off the ground
    from the backward lean. The character looks destabilized — a being of
    shadow momentarily disrupted.


ROW 8 — SHADOW STEP / DODGE (2 frames, holds F1)
---------------------------------
F0: ENTER — body wraps the robe tightly around itself like a cocoon or
    funeral shroud. Arms cross against the chest, pulling the robe inward.
    Scythe is pulled close against the body vertically. Hood dips forward,
    concealing the eyes almost entirely — just the faintest cyan-green
    glimmer at the hood opening. The tattered tendrils at the robe bottom
    curl inward and upward, wrapping around the body. The entire silhouette
    narrows and compresses. The character is dissolving into shadow.

F1: ACTIVE — the character has become an amorphous dark shadow form. The
    shape is a vaguely humanoid blob of darkness (#0F0F1A, #050510) —
    roughly the same height and width as the character but with undefined
    edges that blur and wisp outward. No scythe visible, no hands, no robe
    details — just a dark mass. The two cyan-green eye dots (#00FFAA) float
    in the upper portion of the shadow, still visible — the only recognizable
    feature. The edges of the shadow form have wispy tendrils that fade to
    transparency.
    The engine makes this frame semi-transparent and adds a dark purple tint
    at runtime. Draw the shadow form at full opacity — the engine handles
    the ghosting effect.
    This frame is HELD for 1 full second during the dodge.

IMPORTANT: F1 should NOT look like the character wrapped in a cloak. It
should look like the character has literally BECOME shadow — an indistinct
dark mass with floating eyes. Only the eyes connect it to Mortis.


ROW 9 — ULTIMATE: Death's Harvest (4 frames, one-shot, 200ms)
---------------------------------
F0: CHARGE — body hunches low, almost crouching. Scythe is planted firmly in
    the ground in front with both hands gripping the shaft tightly. Head
    bowed, hood tilted forward. Eyes glow at their most intense — even
    brighter than any other frame, using extra #66FFCC pixels. The chain
    gem blazes with energy. Dark energy is gathering (engine adds the aura
    and swirling particles). Robe whips and lashes as if caught in a
    violent wind — tendrils streaming outward in all directions. The pose
    radiates "power building" — something terrible is about to happen.
    The most tense frame in the sheet.

F1: RISE — body straightens and RISES to maximum height, taller than any
    other frame. Standing at full imposing height. Scythe lifts from the
    ground, rising with the body. Arms beginning to spread. Hood pulls back
    slightly revealing the eyes blazing at full power. Robe billows outward
    dramatically. Shoulder pauldrons' rune markings glow brighter (#00FFAA
    instead of the usual dim #228866). The character is ascending — dark
    power fully charged and rising from the earth. Chain gem glows maximum.

F2: PEAK — the most dramatic pose in the entire sprite sheet. Scythe raised
    above the head with both hands, blade pointing to the right. Body at
    absolute maximum height. Robe SPREADS wide on both sides like massive
    dark wings — filling nearly the full 64px frame width. Hood pulled back,
    eyes blazing twin beacons of cyan-green light. Pauldron runes glowing.
    Arms extended upward and outward, skeletal hands gripping the scythe
    overhead. This is the "angel of death" moment — the apex of power
    before the devastating release. Every visual element is at its most
    extreme — widest robe, brightest eyes, highest scythe, tallest stance.

F3: RELEASE — scythe sweeps in a massive horizontal arc from left to right.
    Body twists powerfully with the swing — the full-body rotation drives
    the scythe. The blade reaches maximum extension to the right. Robe
    whips violently with the rotation, tendrils lashing outward. Eyes
    maintain bright glow. This is the frame where the spectral wave is
    launched — the engine spawns the Death's Harvest wave projectile at the
    scythe blade position. The pose conveys absolute release of all
    accumulated dark energy in one devastating horizontal sweep.

IMPORTANT: The ultimate sequence should be the most cinematic and dramatic
4 frames on the entire sheet. Each frame should feel like a key moment in
a death god's awakening: crouch → rise → ascend → reap.


ROW 10 — DEATH (3 frames, one-shot, 200ms)
---------------------------------
F0: STAGGER — body stumbles forward, listing to the right. Scythe slips from
    the grip, right hand opening — the scythe begins to fall away from the
    body. Left skeletal hand reaches out in front as if grasping for
    something. Eyes FLICKER rapidly — one on, one off, unstable. Hood
    shifts askew. Robe tendrils droop and hang limply (no longer animated
    by supernatural energy). Chain gem's glow gutters. The dark power
    sustaining this being is failing. Knees (hidden by robe) buckle —
    the robe silhouette shows the body lowering and leaning.

F1: FALLING — body tilts sideways to the right, collapsing. Scythe is now
    clearly separated from the body, falling beside or behind the character.
    Arms go limp, skeletal hands hanging. Eyes dim to a faint, sickly
    glow (#228866 — barely visible). Hood falls back, revealing more of
    the void underneath — the darkness under the hood is dissolving,
    becoming less solid. Robe begins to fragment at the edges — tendrils
    break apart into disconnected wisps. The being is literally coming
    undone — the dark magic that holds it together is dissipating.

F2: DOWN — a collapsed pile of tattered robe and the scythe on the ground.
    The body (rotated ~80 degrees clockwise) has lost most of its form —
    what remains is mostly just the robe draped over a vaguely body-shaped
    mound. The scythe lies beside or across the robe pile, blade pointing
    right. The hood has collapsed flat. The eyes are either completely gone
    (extinguished — no glow) OR the faintest single pixel of dim
    cyan (#228866) remains as the last spark. Skeletal hand bones may be
    partially visible poking from a sleeve. Must fit within the 64x64 frame.
    The overall impression: a dark spirit has been vanquished, leaving behind
    only empty robes and a weapon.
