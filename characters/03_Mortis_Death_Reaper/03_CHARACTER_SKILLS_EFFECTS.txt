HOW HYBRID EFFECTS WORK
================================================================================

  Your Sprite Art    +    Engine Particles    =    Final In-Game Look
  (shape & style)         (glow, trail, sparks)    (polished & dynamic)

You draw the CORE shape. The engine adds glow, trails, sparks at runtime.
Your sprites do NOT need trailing particles or ambient glow — just the main shape.

      cyan-green energy, ~20-24px diameter, centered in the 32x32 frame.
      Hollow dark eye sockets (#050510 void inside). Open mouth in a silent

      ethereal — not a solid bone skull but a GHOSTLY skull made of energy.
      The edges of the skull wisp and blur slightly, like it's made of

      appears at the edge of each socket. The front edge of the skull sharpens
      while the back edge dissolves more into smoke. Overall diameter
      increases 2-3px. This is the most aggressive frame — the skull looks
      like it's REACHING for the target.

  F3: Skull contracts back toward F0 shape. Mouth closes slightly. Eyes
      return to void. Edges smooth out, wisps settle. Diameter shrinks back.
      Returns seamlessly to F0 for the loop.

  Core (brightest)  : #CCFFEE (white-mint), #66FFCC (bright mint)
  Skull body        : #00FFAA (spectral cyan), #00CC88 (ghost green)
  Skull edges       : #008866 (teal), #004433 (deep teal)
  Eye sockets       : #050510 (void black)
  Vapor wisps       : #004433 (deep teal), #221133 (dark purple-gray)

Design Notes:
  - This is a GHOSTLY skull, not a realistic bone skull. Think "spectral
    energy that has taken the shape of a screaming skull."
  - The skull should glow — brightest at the crown/forehead, dimming toward
    the edges and jaw.
  - Leave 4-5px padding around the skull for the flickering edges.
  - Eye sockets are DARK VOIDS in a glowing shape — strong contrast.
  - The engine adds a shadow vapor trail behind, so only draw small wisps
    at the back edge of the skull itself.
  - Passes through enemy projectiles in-game — it is spectral, intangible
    to other energy but solid to players.

>>> THIS IS A GROUND-PLACED EFFECT — NOT A SHIELD/AURA <<<

This is NOT centered on the player. It is placed ON THE GROUND at the cast
position and stays there for 3 seconds. It does NOT have an empty center
for the player to show through. The entire 96x96 frame can contain art.

The circle is viewed from the game's SIDE-VIEW camera perspective. This means
the magic circle on the ground appears as a perspective ELLIPSE (wider than
tall) — similar to how a manhole cover looks from the side. It is NOT a
perfect circle.

Structure (side view perspective):
  +----------------------------+
  |                            |
  |     . .  rune  . .         |    <- Faint floating rune symbols above
  |    .    glow    .          |       the circle (spectral, 3-4px tall)
  |   ╔══════════════╗         |
  |   ║  RUNE  RING  ║         |    <- Outer runic ring (ellipse ~80x24px)
  |   ║ ╔══════════╗ ║         |       Glowing cyan-green rune markings
  |   ║ ║  INNER   ║ ║         |    <- Inner circle area (darker, pulsing)
  |   ║ ╚══════════╝ ║         |
  |   ╚══════════════╝         |
  |  ~ ~ ~ ground glow ~ ~ ~  |    <- Ground-level glow beneath the circle
  +----------------------------+
       y=96 (ground line)

The circle sits in the LOWER HALF of the 96x96 frame. The upper half
contains floating rune symbols and wisps that rise from the circle.

Frame Guide:
  F0: Dark magic circle on the ground — viewed in side perspective as a
      horizontal ellipse. Outer ring (~80px wide, ~24px tall) made of
      connected runic symbols and arcane markings in spectral cyan (#00FFAA).
      Inner area is darker (#0F0F1A, #050510) with a faint pulsing glow.
      The runes are angular, geometric, occult-looking symbols — NOT
      readable text. Ground beneath the circle has a faint green underglow
      (#004433, semi-transparent). Above the circle, 2-3 small floating
      rune symbols drift upward (3-4px tall, in #008866 dim green).
      Steady state — the trap is active and waiting.

  F1: Runes pulse BRIGHTER — the outer ring glows more intensely (#66FFCC
      highlights appear on the rune markings). The inner circle area shows
      a ripple of energy moving from center outward (a ring of #00CC88
      expanding within the dark inner area). Floating rune symbols above
      drift slightly higher and rotate. Ground underglow intensifies
      slightly. The circle is pulsing — drawing energy.

  F2: PEAK GLOW — runes at maximum brightness. From the edges of the outer
      ring, faint spectral hands (#00FFAA, #008866) reach UPWARD — 3-4
      ghostly skeletal fingers rising from the circle rim, ~8-12px tall.
      These represent souls being pulled from the ground. The inner circle
      shows maximum energy ripple. Floating rune symbols are at their
      highest position. Ground underglow at maximum. This is the most
      dramatic and unsettling frame — the circle is actively draining.

  F3: Glow dims back toward F0 levels. Spectral hands recede — shrinking
      back down into the circle edge, dissolving. Energy ripple in the inner
      circle fades. Floating runes drift back down. Ground underglow dims.
      Smooth return to F0 for seamless loop.

Color Palette:
  Rune ring (base)    : #00FFAA (spectral cyan)
  Rune ring (bright)  : #66FFCC (bright mint), #CCFFEE (white-mint on peak)
  Rune ring (dim)     : #00CC88 (ghost green), #008866 (teal)
  Inner circle        : #0F0F1A (near-black), #050510 (void)
  Energy ripple       : #00CC88 (ghost green)
  Spectral hands      : #00FFAA (cyan), #008866 (teal), #004433 (deep teal)
  Ground underglow    : #004433 (deep teal, semi-transparent)
  Floating runes      : #008866 (teal), #228866 (dim green)

Design Notes:
  - The circle is a TRAP — it should look dangerous and ominous on the ground.
  - The perspective ellipse shape is critical — a perfect circle would look
    wrong in a side-view game. Width should be ~3x the height.
  - The spectral hands in F2 are subtle and small — they suggest "something
    reaching from below" without being too literal. Just ghostly finger shapes.
  - The floating rune symbols above add vertical interest and show that
    the circle has an active magical field extending upward.
  - Enemies standing in this circle take 5 dmg/second and are slowed 30%.

TIPS FOR AI IMAGE GENERATION
================================================================================

1. If AI gives individual images instead of a sprite sheet:
   Generate each frame separately, then stitch side-by-side in
   Photopea.com (free browser Photoshop) or even MS Paint.

2. To remove backgrounds:
   Use remove.bg, or Photopea "Magic Wand + Delete",
   or GIMP "Color to Alpha".

3. To resize to exact dimensions:
   Photopea -> Image -> Canvas Size -> set exact pixels.
   Use "Nearest Neighbor" resampling to preserve pixel art.

4. To keep style consistent:
   Upload your Mortis character sprite as a style reference
   (if your AI tool supports reference images).

5. Frame alignment:
   If frames are different sizes, create new canvas at total size,
   paste each frame at correct X position:
   Frame 0 at x=0, Frame 1 at x=frameWidth, Frame 2 at x=frameWidth*2...

6. DARK PALETTE WARNING:
   AI generators tend to make things brighter than requested. You may
   need to darken the results in post-processing. The void areas should
   be VERY dark (#050510, #0F0F1A) — almost black. The only bright
   elements are the cyan-green edges and glows.
